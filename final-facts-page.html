Greenhouse Gas Emissions

  • According to a United Nations report, animal agriculture is responsible for 18% of greenhouse gas emissions, which is more than the entire transportation sector combined, which only contributes to around 13% of greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Some reports put animal agriculture's CO2 contribution as high as 51%.
  • Methane is the primary greenhouse gas emitted by animals, and is 25-100 times more destructive that CO2.

Land/Water Use

  • Animal Agriculture is responsible for 80% of water consumption.
  • Growing feed for livestock consumes 56% of the water in the U.S. alone.
  • It takes 2,500 gallons of water to produce 1 pound of beef, and around 1,000 gallons of water to produce 1 gallon of milk.
  • Livestock or feed for livestock ocupies 1/3 of the earth's land
  • Livestock on land has created more than 500 Nitrogen flooded dead zones in the world's oceans.

Rainforest Destruction

  • Animal agriculture is responsible for up to 91% of amazon rainforest destruction.
  • One acre of rainforest is cleared every second.
  • This destruction is primarily due to livestock and feed crops, and leads to great animal, plant, and insect loss.

Waste

  • In the U.S, livestock produces 116,000 pounds of waste per second.
  • Animals produce 1.4 billion tons of waste from the meat industry in the U.S.
  • Globally, enough animal waste is produced to cover an area greater than San Francisco, New York City, Tokyo, etc combined.

Source: cowspiracy.com
