Greenhouse Gas Emissions

According to a United Nations report, animal agriculture is responsible for 18% of greenhouse gas emissions, which is more than the entire transportation sector combined, which only contributes to around 13% of greenhouse gas emissions.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Some reports put animal agriculture's CO2 contribution as high as 51%.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Methane is the primary greenhouse gas emitted by animals, and is 25-100 times more destructive than CO2.

Land and Water Use

Animal Agriculture is responsible for 80% of water consumption. Growing feed for livestock consumes 56% of the water in the U.S. alone.

Land and Water Use

It takes 2,500 gallons of water to produce 1 pound of beef, and around 1,000 gallons of water to produce 1 gallon of milk.

Land and Water Use

Livestock or feed for livestock ocupies 1/3 of the earth's land, and livestock have created more than 500 Nitrogen flooded dead zones in the world's oceans.

Rainforest Destruction

Animal agriculture is responsible for up to 91% of amazon rainforest destruction.

Rainforest Destruction

One acre of rainforest is cleared every second.

Rainforest Destruction

This rinforest destruction is primarily due to livestock grazing and feed crops grown for livestock, and leads to great animal, plant, and insect loss.
